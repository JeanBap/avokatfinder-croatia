Uporabna Dozvola

The uporabna dozvola is the use permit (certificate of occupancy) issued in Croatia after a building passes final inspection, certifying that the completed structure complies with its building permit and is safe and legal to occupy.

Once construction is finished under a valid građevinska dozvola, the building must undergo a technical inspection (tehnički pregled) carried out by a commission including the supervising engineer, fire inspector, sanitary inspector, and representatives of any utility providers. If the inspection confirms compliance with the permit and applicable technical regulations, the authority issues the uporabna dozvola.

The use permit is the legal gateway to residential or commercial occupation. Without it, the property cannot be legally inhabited or used for business, cannot be connected to utility networks on a permanent basis, and cannot be registered in the land registry as a unit ready for ownership transfer.

Buyers of new-build properties from developers must receive a copy of the uporabna dozvola at completion. The permit is also required for the notarial deed to proceed; without it, the property's legal status is incomplete and the buyer cannot be entered in the land registry as owner.

Properties constructed under the regularisation law (Zakon o postupanju s nezakonito izgrađenim zgradama) from 2011–2013 received a rješenje o izvedenom stanju — a regularisation decision — that functioned as a retrospective use permit. Buyers of such properties should verify that the regularisation certificate has been registered in both the Cadastre and the Land Registry.

Some older rural properties were built under simple construction notifications (prijava radova) and did not require a full use permit under then-applicable law. For these, buyers should confirm via the local authority that no enforcement proceedings are pending and that the building is legally habitable.

⚠ Common MistakeSome developers hand over keys before the use permit is issued, relying on assurances it will follow 'within weeks'. Moving into a property without a use permit is legally irregular and can affect mortgage drawdown. Insist on receiving the permit before completion.
💡 Expert TipWhen reviewing new-build purchase contracts, ensure the delivery obligation is tied to receipt of the uporabna dozvola, not just physical construction completion. Include a penalty clause and right of rescission if the developer cannot obtain the permit within a defined longstop date.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I occupy a property before the use permit is issued?

Legally no — occupying without a use permit is a regulatory offence and can be sanctioned by the State Inspectorate, though enforcement in residential cases is infrequent.

Is the use permit the same as a habitation permit in other countries?

It is equivalent to a certificate of occupancy or habitation permit. It confirms legal compliance, not just physical completion.

What if my new-build developer becomes insolvent before obtaining the use permit?

You may need to appoint an administrator or apply to court to complete the inspection process. Legal advice specific to Croatian insolvency and construction law is essential in this scenario.
