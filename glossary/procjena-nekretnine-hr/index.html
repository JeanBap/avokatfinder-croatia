Procjena Nekretnine

Procjena nekretnine is the professional valuation of a property in Croatia carried out by a certified appraiser to determine its market value for purchase, mortgage, tax, or legal purposes.

A procjena nekretnine (property appraisal or valuation) is a formal written opinion of a property's market value prepared by a licensed appraiser (ovlašteni procjenitelj). Banks require an independent valuation before approving a mortgage; courts require one in succession, divorce, and compulsory purchase proceedings; and the tax authority uses valuations to assess if consideration is below market value.

Croatian appraisers must hold a licence from the Ministry of Finance and apply standards aligned with the International Valuation Standards (IVS) and, increasingly, RICS guidance. The valuation report details the property's physical characteristics, legal status, comparable sales, and the methodology used — typically the comparative market approach for residential properties.

The valuation fee is typically borne by the buyer or mortgagor. Banks often have a panel of approved appraisers; buyers generally cannot select their own valuer for a mortgage valuation unless the bank permits it. Independent appraisers may be engaged by either party for non-bank purposes.

A bank valuation (bankovna procjena) serves the lender's interests and may be more conservative than an open-market valuation. Buyers should not confuse the bank's valuation with an independent market assessment. A lower bank valuation means the mortgage offered may be less than expected, requiring a higher equity contribution.

The Croatian Real Estate Act and the Valuation of Real Estate Act regulate who may issue valuations for official purposes. Only licensed members of the Croatian Valuers Register may prepare reports accepted by banks, courts, and the tax authority.

⚠ Common MistakeBuyers sometimes assume the asking price equals market value. In illiquid or rural markets, asking prices can diverge significantly from appraised values. Always obtain an independent valuation before making an offer, especially for properties priced above recent comparables.
💡 Expert TipCommission a separate private valuation in addition to the bank's. A private report from a senior RICS-affiliated appraiser gives you negotiating evidence and flags technical issues the bank's valuer may not report because they are acting for the lender, not for you.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is a valuation required by law for all property sales?

Not for private sales between willing parties. It is required for mortgage lending, probate, compulsory purchase, and certain tax assessments.

Can I use the local government's assessed value instead?

The porezna vrijednost (tax-assessed value) is often lower than market value and is not accepted by banks as a market valuation. It is used only for calculating transfer tax.

How long does a valuation take?

Typically 5–10 working days from the site inspection, depending on complexity and the appraiser's workload.
