Građevinska Dozvola

Građevinska dozvola is the building permit issued by Croatian county or state building authorities that legally authorises construction, extension, or major reconstruction of a building before any work commences.

A građevinska dozvola (building permit) is mandatory in Croatia for the construction of new buildings, substantial extensions, structural alterations, and changes of use. It is issued by the county office (ureda državne uprave) or, for complex structures, the State Inspectorate. Applications are submitted under the Construction Act (Zakon o gradnji).

The permit application requires a main design (glavni projekt) prepared by a licensed architect or engineer, confirmation that the project conforms to the spatial plan (prostorni plan) of the municipality, a location permit (lokacijska dozvola) for certain types of projects, land ownership evidence, and proof that komunalni doprinos has been settled.

Once a building permit is issued, construction must commence within three years; otherwise the permit lapses. During construction, the work must be supervised by a licensed building supervisor (nadzorni inženjer) who certifies that the works conform to the permit and design. The final step is the use permit (uporabna dozvola).

Buyers of properties without a valid building permit face serious legal exposure. Unpermitted buildings cannot be registered in the land registry as separate units, cannot be mortgaged, and may be subject to demolition orders issued by the State Inspectorate. The 2011–2013 regularisation process (ozakonjenje) legalised many illegal structures, but properties built after that window generally cannot be regularised.

Even permitted buildings may have subsequent unpermitted additions — extensions, outbuildings, or floor conversions. Buyers should compare the physical structure against the registered permit plans and the land registry record. A surveyor and a property lawyer should assess any discrepancies before purchase.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between a building permit and a use permit?

The građevinska dozvola authorises construction to begin. The uporabna dozvola confirms the completed building complies with the permit and is safe to occupy. Both must exist for a legally compliant property.

Can I obtain a copy of the building permit for a property I am buying?

Yes. Permits are public documents held by the issuing authority. Your solicitor or you can request a certified copy from the county office.

What happens if the building permit expires before construction starts?

The permit lapses and a new application must be submitted. Projects started without a valid permit are illegal and subject to enforcement action.
