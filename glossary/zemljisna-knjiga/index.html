Zemljišna Knjiga

Zemljišna Knjiga is the Croatian Land Registry — the official public register recording all real property ownership, mortgages, easements, and other encumbrances for properties in Croatia.

The Zemljišna Knjiga is maintained by the Croatian courts (općinski sudovi) and is the definitive legal record of property rights in Croatia. Registration is constitutive — ownership only vests upon entry in the Land Registry, not upon signing the purchase contract.

The Land Registry is accessible online via the e-Oglasna Ploca portal (oss.uredjenazemlja.hr), allowing free searches by cadastral municipality and lot number. Each property entry contains the ownership section (B list) and the encumbrance section (C list).

A predbilježba (preliminary registration) can be entered while the full documentation is being assembled, protecting the buyer's priority during the processing period. It converts to a full bilježba (registration) once all conditions are met.

Before any Croatian property purchase, buyers must obtain a fresh zemljišnoknjižni izvadak (Land Registry extract) to verify the seller's ownership and check the C list for hipoteke (mortgages), pljenidbe (attachments), and any zabrane otuđenja (restrictions on alienation).

Common Mistake: Relying on a Zemljišna Knjiga extract from earlier in the transaction rather than pulling a same-day extract before signing. Encumbrances can be registered quickly — a week-old extract may miss a mortgage registered by the seller days later.
Expert Tip: Use the e-Oglasna Ploca portal to pull a fresh Land Registry extract the morning of signing. The portal is free, real-time, and requires no registration. Make this the last check before funds are released.

The Katastar (cadastre) and Zemljišna Knjiga are separate but complementary records in Croatia. The cadastre records physical measurements; the land registry records legal rights. Harmonisation between the two systems is ongoing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Croatian Land Registry publicly accessible?

Yes. The Zemljišna Knjiga is freely accessible online via the e-Oglasna Ploca portal at oss.uredjenazemlja.hr. Anyone can search by cadastral municipality and parcel number to view ownership and encumbrances.

What is a predbilježba in Croatian property law?

A predbilježba is a preliminary registration in the Zemljišna Knjiga entered when documentation is incomplete. It protects the buyer's priority while the full paperwork is assembled, converting to a full registration (bilježba) once conditions are satisfied.

What does the C list show in the Croatian Land Registry?

The C list of the Zemljišna Knjiga records all encumbrances including hipoteke (mortgages), pljenidbe (judicial attachments), easements (služnosti), and zabrane otuđenja (restrictions on alienation or mortgaging).
