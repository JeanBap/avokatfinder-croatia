Kupoprodajni Ugovor (Purchase Contract)

The Croatian real estate purchase contract — the binding agreement transferring ownership of property between seller and buyer.

Home › Legal Glossary › Kupoprodajni Ugovor (Purchase Contract)
TL;DR The Croatian real estate purchase contract — the binding agreement transferring ownership of property between seller and buyer.

The kupoprodajni ugovor is the sale and purchase agreement that transfers legal title to real estate in Croatia. It must be notarially authenticated (solemnised or authenticated by a javni bilježnik) before it can be submitted to the gruntovnica (land registry) to effect the transfer of ownership. A private written contract is binding between the parties but insufficient to complete registration.

A valid kupoprodajni ugovor must identify the parties with full personal details and OIB numbers, precisely describe the property using cadastral references (katastarska čestica and land registry insert number), state the agreed purchase price, include the tabular declaration (tabularna izjava) authorising registry entry, and be signed by both parties in the presence of a notary. Missing any of these elements will cause the land registry to reject the application.

Croatian law implies certain seller warranties into every property sale unless explicitly excluded. The seller warrants that the property is free from undisclosed encumbrances, that they have the right to sell, and that the property conforms to its described condition. Buyers should still conduct thorough due diligence — implied warranties are enforceable but litigation is time-consuming and expensive.

Foreign buyers often use a preliminary agreement (predugovor) before the main kupoprodajni ugovor. The predugovor locks in the price and key terms while the buyer arranges financing or completes due diligence checks. It is typically accompanied by a deposit of 10% of the purchase price. Breaking the predugovor without legal cause entitles the innocent party to either enforce the transaction or claim double the deposit.

After signing the kupoprodajni ugovor, the buyer must pay real estate transfer tax (porez na promet nekretnina) at 3% of market value within 30 days, obtain a tax clearance certificate, and submit the completed documentation to the gruntovnica. Failure to complete registration within the prescribed period may result in administrative penalties and loss of priority against subsequent purchasers.

⚠ Common Mistake: Signing the kupoprodajni ugovor without obtaining an up-to-date gruntovnica extract and energy performance certificate (energetski certifikat). Both are legally required and their absence can delay or void the transaction.
💡 Expert Tip: Include a suspensive condition (odgodni uvjet) in the contract if your purchase depends on mortgage approval. This allows you to withdraw and recover your deposit if financing falls through, rather than forfeiting the predugovor deposit.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can the contract be in English?

The notarially authenticated version must be in Croatian. An English translation may accompany it for reference, but only the Croatian text has legal effect. Hire a certified translator to review the Croatian version before signing.

What happens if the seller backs out?

If the seller withdraws without legal justification after signing the predugovor, the buyer can demand return of double the deposit. If the main kupoprodajni ugovor is signed, the buyer can seek specific performance (prisilno ispunjenje) through the courts.

Is VAT charged on property sales?

New residential buildings sold by a developer (first sale within two years of construction) are subject to VAT at 25% instead of the 3% transfer tax. Resale of existing properties is subject to the 3% transfer tax, not VAT.
