Predujam (Kapara)

Predujam or kapara is the deposit paid by a buyer when signing a preliminary property purchase agreement in Croatia, typically 10% of the purchase price, that commits both parties to completing the transaction.

In Croatian practice, the preliminary agreement (predugovor) is signed before the main notarised sale contract (kupoprodajni ugovor). At this stage the buyer pays a predujam — commonly called kapara in everyday usage — to demonstrate commitment and reserve the property.

Croatian contract law provides strong protections around the deposit. If the buyer withdraws from the transaction without legal justification, the seller keeps the entire kapara. Conversely, if the seller withdraws, they must return double the amount received — a rule that incentivises sellers to honour the agreement.

The predujam is typically set at 10% of the agreed purchase price, though parties may negotiate a different amount. The funds should be paid to the seller directly or held in escrow by a notary or solicitor, with a clear written record in the preliminary agreement.

The predugovor should specify the deadline for signing the main contract, the exact purchase price, conditions precedent (such as mortgage approval or planning confirmation), and consequences of default by either party. A property lawyer should review this document before any money changes hands.

If the sale falls through due to a third-party circumstance covered in the agreement — for example, the buyer's mortgage application being refused despite best efforts — the contract may allow the deposit to be returned. Specific wording is critical, so legal advice before signing is strongly recommended.

⚠ Common MistakeBuyers occasionally transfer the deposit without a signed predugovor, relying on verbal assurances. This is extremely risky — without a written agreement, enforcing return of funds is difficult if the seller changes their mind.
💡 Expert TipHave a property lawyer draft or review the predugovor before payment. Confirm that the land registry is free of encumbrances, and include a clause allowing deposit return if your mortgage is declined through no fault of your own.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the predujam the same as an advance payment?

No. A predujam/kapara is a deposit with forfeiture consequences. An ordinary advance payment (avans) is simply returned if the deal falls through, with no double-return obligation on the seller.

What if the property has a hidden defect discovered after paying the deposit?

Material hidden defects discovered after the predugovor may entitle the buyer to rescind the agreement and recover the deposit. A professional building survey before signing is strongly advised.

Does the notary hold the kapara?

Not automatically. The predugovor may stipulate notary escrow, but in many cases the seller receives funds directly. Your solicitor can arrange escrow if preferred.
