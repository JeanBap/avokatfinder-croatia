Pravo Prvokupa

Pravo prvokupa (right of first refusal or pre-emption right) is a legal right in Croatian property law that entitles a specific party — such as a co-owner or the state — to purchase a property before the seller can sell to a third party.

Pravo prvokupa operates as a pre-emption right: the holder must be offered the property on the same terms as any third-party offer before the sale can proceed. In Croatia, this right arises by law in several situations and can also be created contractually.

The most common statutory right of first refusal applies among co-owners (suvlasnici). If a co-owner wishes to sell their undivided share, they must first offer it to the other co-owners at the same price and conditions offered by a prospective external buyer. Co-owners have 30 days to exercise the right; failure to respond is treated as a waiver.

The Croatian state holds a pravo prvokupa over agricultural land under the Agricultural Land Act (Zakon o poljoprivrednom zemljištu). Before selling agricultural land, the seller must notify the local agricultural administration, which may exercise the state's pre-emption right within 30 days at the market price agreed with the buyer.

In coastal areas, municipalities may hold a right of first refusal over certain maritime domain properties. In protected natural areas and cultural heritage zones, additional pre-emption rights may exist in favour of conservation bodies. Sellers must identify all pre-emption rights before marketing a property.

If a seller fails to respect an active pravo prvokupa and completes a sale to a third party, the right holder can challenge the transaction in court and demand the sale be declared invalid or that they be substituted as the buyer at the same price — a remedy known as tužba radi ostvarenja prava prvokupa.

⚠ Common MistakeSellers sometimes market and sell co-owned property without notifying co-owners of their first refusal right. The overlooked co-owner can then apply to court to have the sale set aside, creating major legal and financial complications for the innocent buyer.
💡 Expert TipBefore signing any purchase agreement on a property with multiple co-owners or agricultural classification, have a solicitor identify all existing pravo prvokupa obligations and obtain written waivers from every right holder. This prevents challenges after completion.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does pravo prvokupa apply to inheritances?

Generally no — statutory pre-emption rights are triggered by sale, not inheritance. However, a contractually agreed pravo prvokupa may include inheritance as a trigger if explicitly stated.

Can pravo prvokupa be sold or transferred?

A contractual right of first refusal may be assignable if the contract permits. Statutory rights (e.g. co-owner rights) are personal and cannot be transferred.

What if two co-owners both want to exercise their right?

If more than one co-owner wishes to buy, they acquire the offered share proportionally to their existing shares, unless the contract or court orders otherwise.
