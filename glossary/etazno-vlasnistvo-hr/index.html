Etažno Vlasništvo

Etažno vlasništvo is the Croatian legal framework governing ownership of individual apartments and units within a multi-unit building, combining exclusive ownership of the private unit with shared ownership of common areas.

Under Croatian property law, etažno vlasništvo (literally 'floor ownership') allows a person to own a specific apartment, office, or garage space within a larger building as a separate legal unit. Each unit is registered independently in the land registry (zemljišna knjiga) with its own title deed.

The regime comprises two elements: exclusive ownership of the private space (stan, poslovni prostor, or garaža) and a proportional co-ownership share in the common parts of the building — staircases, roof, load-bearing walls, lifts, and land underneath. This share is calculated based on the unit's floor area relative to the total building area.

Etažno vlasništvo is established through a decision of all co-owners (etažni vlasnici) or by court judgment, then registered in the Cadastre and Land Registry. The building must first have a usable permit (uporabna dozvola), confirming it complies with building regulations.

A mandatory body called the zajednica etažnih vlasnika (community of apartment owners) manages the building's common areas and collects pričuva — monthly reserve fund contributions used for maintenance and repairs. Larger buildings must appoint an upravitelj (property manager) licensed by the Ministry of Physical Planning.

Buyers should examine the building's pričuva balance and any outstanding major repair decisions before purchase, as debts can transfer to new owners in practice. A solicitor can obtain these records from the existing management company.

⚠ Common MistakeBuyers sometimes assume the listed price covers all costs. In etažno vlasništvo, unpaid pričuva arrears from the previous owner may become a practical burden — always request a certified statement of outstanding charges.
💡 Expert TipAsk the building manager for the last three years of pričuva statements and any adopted maintenance plans. Upcoming lift replacements or roof works can cost tens of thousands of euros and must be funded collectively.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between etažno vlasništvo and suvlasništvo?

Etažno vlasništvo grants exclusive ownership of a defined unit; suvlasništvo is general undivided co-ownership of an entire property without exclusive portions.

Can a foreigner own an etažno vlasništvo unit?

Yes. EU citizens have the same rights as Croatian nationals. Non-EU nationals may need reciprocity or special approvals depending on their country.

How is the monthly pričuva calculated?

The Law on Maintenance of Buildings sets a minimum rate per m² of usable floor area. Building managers may propose higher contributions if the building's condition demands it.
