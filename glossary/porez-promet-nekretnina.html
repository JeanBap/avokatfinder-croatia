Porez na Promet Nekretnina

Croatia's property transfer tax — percentage-based tax on real estate transactions.

Overview

Porez na Promet Nekretnina (Property Transfer Tax) is Croatia's mandatory tax on the transfer of real estate ownership. The standard rate is 5% of the property's declared or assessed value, depending on the transaction type and parties' status. EU citizens and Croatian residents typically pay the standard 5% rate. Certain transfers (between spouses, direct descendants/ascendants, or charitable donations) may qualify for reduced rates (1-3%) or exemptions.

The tax base is the property's market value on the transfer date, or the declared value in the contract if higher. Tax authorities can challenge significantly undervalued declarations and assess properties at estimated market value. The Croatian Tax Administration conducts periodic reviews and comparisons to detect undervaluation schemes.

The buyer typically pays the property transfer tax, though this may be negotiated in the purchase contract. Payment is made to the Local Tax Office (Porezna Uprava) and proof of payment must be provided to the Land Registry before registration can be completed. Unpaid transfer tax prevents property registration, making the transaction invalid.

Exemptions are available for transfers between spouses, transfers to direct descendants or ascendants, and donations to qualified charitable organizations. Each exemption requires specific documentation (marriage certificate, birth certificate, charitable organization registration). Transfers by inheritance are subject to inheritance tax instead of transfer tax, with different rates.

Tax evasion through undervaluation is subject to substantial penalties, interest charges, and potential criminal liability. Professional property appraisals and comparable sales data help defend declared values against tax authority challenges. Recent enforcement efforts have increased scrutiny of property valuations, particularly in major cities.

Key Facts

  • 5% standard rate for property transfer (buyer typically pays)
  • Reduced rates (1-3%) for family transfers and charitable donations
  • Calculated on market value or declared value (whichever is higher)
  • Exemptions available with proper documentation of relationship or charity status
  • Must be paid and documented before Land Registry registration

Common Mistake

Undervaluing property to minimize taxes — tax authorities actively enforce valuations and impose severe penalties for undervaluation.

Pro Tip

Obtain a professional property appraisal to support your declared value and provide evidence against tax authority challenges.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are there reduced rates for family property transfers?

Yes. Transfers between spouses, direct descendants/ascendants, or to charitable organizations typically qualify for reduced rates (0-1%) or exemptions with proper documentation proving the relationship or charitable status.

What happens if I don't pay the transfer tax?

The property cannot be registered in the Land Registry. The transaction remains invalid until the tax is paid. Unpaid taxes accumulate interest and penalties over time.

Is inheritance subject to the same transfer tax?

No. Inherited property is subject to inheritance tax (Porez na Nasljeđivanje), which has different rates based on the heir's relationship to the deceased. Direct descendants typically pay 1-5%, while more distant relatives pay higher rates.
