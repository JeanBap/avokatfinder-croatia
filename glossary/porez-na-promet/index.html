Porez na Promet Nekretnina

Porez na promet nekretnina is the Croatian real estate transfer tax of 3% — levied on the buyer on resale property transactions and assessed by the Croatian Tax Administration on the market value.

Croatia's real estate transfer tax is payable by the buyer at 3% of the market value of the property, or the purchase price if higher. The tax is administered by the Porezna Uprava (Croatian Tax Administration) and must be paid within 30 days of the signed purchase contract.

The tax does not apply to new-build sales by VAT-registered developers — in that case, 25% VAT applies instead. Buyers must determine which regime applies before calculating total acquisition costs. Mixed-use properties may have parts subject to VAT and parts subject to the transfer tax.

If the Porezna Uprava considers the declared purchase price below market value, it may revalue the property and assess the 3% tax on the higher amount. Buyers can appeal within 30 days of the assessment. Declaring the genuine transaction price avoids this risk.

Certain Croatian property transfers are exempt from the transfer tax, including transfers between close family members in certain inheritance-related scenarios and some restructuring transactions. An exemption application must be filed with the Porezna Uprava.

Common Mistake: Assuming the 3% transfer tax applies to new-build Croatian property. New builds sold by developers are subject to 25% VAT — a far higher burden. Always confirm whether the property is a resale or a new build before calculating acquisition costs.
Expert Tip: Pay the transfer tax promptly within the 30-day window. Late payment attracts default interest at statutory rates. The Zemljišna Knjiga will not process registration without confirmation of tax payment.

Budget 3% for porez na promet nekretnina alongside notarial fees (typically 0.5–1% of price), land registry fees (around HRK 200 fixed plus a percentage), and legal fees when calculating the total cost of acquiring Croatian real estate.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who pays the real estate transfer tax in Croatia?

The buyer (primatelj) pays the porez na promet nekretnina at 3% of the higher of the purchase price or the market value assessed by the Porezna Uprava. Payment is due within 30 days of the signed kupoprodajni ugovor.

What is the VAT rate on new Croatian property?

New-build residential property sold by a VAT-registered developer is subject to 25% Croatian VAT. This replaces the 3% transfer tax. The significantly higher rate makes new builds considerably more expensive in tax terms.

Can the Croatian Tax Authority reassess a declared purchase price?

Yes. If the Porezna Uprava considers the declared price materially below market value, it can issue a revalued assessment and charge the 3% transfer tax on the higher figure. The parties have 30 days to appeal.
