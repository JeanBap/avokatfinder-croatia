Gruntovnica (Land Registry)

The official Croatian land registry that records property ownership, mortgages, and encumbrances on real estate.

Home › Legal Glossary › Gruntovnica (Land Registry)
TL;DR The official Croatian land registry that records property ownership, mortgages, and encumbrances on real estate.

The gruntovnica, or land registry, is the authoritative public record of property rights in Croatia. Maintained by municipal courts, it documents who owns each parcel of land, what mortgages or liens are attached, and any easements or restrictions that apply. Every property transaction in Croatia must be registered here to be legally effective against third parties.

Foreign nationals purchasing property in Croatia must register their ownership in the gruntovnica within 30 days of completing the sale. The registration process involves submitting a certified copy of the purchase contract, proof of payment of real estate transfer tax, and an application to the relevant municipal court. Until registration is complete, the buyer's ownership is not publicly visible and remains vulnerable to competing claims.

The gruntovnica is divided into three main ledgers. The posjedovnica (possession ledger) describes the physical characteristics of the land parcel. The vlastovnica (ownership ledger) records the current owner and their legal basis of title. The teretovnica (encumbrance ledger) lists all mortgages, easements, pre-emption rights, and other charges on the property.

Since 2013, Croatia's land registry has been largely digitised and accessible through the e-Gruntovnica online portal. Searches can be conducted by cadastral parcel number (katastarska čestica) or by owner name. The extract (izvadak iz gruntovnice) costs around HRK 40–80 and is accepted as official evidence of ownership in legal proceedings, bank applications, and notarial deeds.

Always verify that the seller listed in the gruntovnica matches the person signing the purchase contract. Discrepancies — such as an estate not yet transferred to heirs — can delay or invalidate the transaction. A qualified Croatian property lawyer or notary (javni bilježnik) should review the gruntovnica extract before any purchase agreement is signed.

Registration deadline30 days from the date of the purchase contract
Three ledgersPosjedovnica (land), Vlastovnica (ownership), Teretovnica (charges)
Transfer tax linkTax clearance (potvrda o plaćenom porezu) required before registration
⚠ Common Mistake: Paying the purchase price before checking the teretovnica (encumbrance ledger). Hidden mortgages or tax liens transfer with the property — they do not automatically disappear on sale unless specifically discharged.
💡 Expert Tip: Request a gruntovnica extract dated no more than seven days before signing the purchase contract. Property records can change quickly; a stale extract may miss a recently registered mortgage.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can foreigners access the gruntovnica?

Yes. The e-Gruntovnica portal is publicly accessible. Any person can search for a property extract by parcel number. No Croatian residency or citizenship is required to view or purchase official extracts.

How long does registration take?

Standard registration typically takes 30–60 days at municipal courts. Priority registration (hitna uknjižba) can be requested for an additional fee and may be processed within a few days for time-sensitive transactions.

What is a ZK extract and why do I need one?

A ZK extract (izvadak iz zemljišne knjige) is the official certified printout from the gruntovnica. It is required by banks for mortgage applications, by notaries for property transfers, and by courts in ownership disputes. It serves as prima facie evidence of the registered state of the property.
