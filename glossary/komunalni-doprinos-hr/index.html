Komunalni Doprinos

Komunalni doprinos is a one-time infrastructure contribution levied by Croatian municipalities on new construction and significant extensions, payable before a building permit is issued, to fund public infrastructure such as roads, sewers, and parks.

When a developer or individual applies for a building permit (građevinska dozvola) in Croatia, the relevant municipality charges a komunalni doprinos — a development contribution — to fund the capital costs of public infrastructure serving the new building. The charge is set by the municipality and varies significantly by location and zone.

The amount is calculated by multiplying the usable floor area (korisna površina) by a rate per square metre that reflects the zone's infrastructure development level. Prime urban zones command higher rates; rural or peripheral zones lower ones. Municipalities publish their rates in local decisions (odluka o komunalnom doprinosu).

Komunalni doprinos must be paid before the building permit is issued. Municipalities may allow payment in instalments, but the permit is only issued once the obligation is settled or secured. Failure to pay is a criminal offence that can lead to demolition orders in egregious cases.

Buyers of new-build properties should confirm that the developer has paid komunalni doprinos in full, as unpaid infrastructure contributions can create legal complications. The payment receipt is part of the documentation required for the completion certificate (uporabna dozvola) and for land registry entry.

Komunalni doprinos should not be confused with komunalna naknada, which is a recurring annual municipal charge for maintaining existing infrastructure paid by all property owners. The doprinos is a one-off build charge; the naknada is an annual maintenance levy.

⚠ Common MistakeOff-plan buyers sometimes assume the developer has paid komunalni doprinos. Always request documentary evidence — the payment receipt and confirmation from the municipality. Projects that stall mid-build are sometimes found to have unpaid infrastructure obligations.
💡 Expert TipAsk for the municipality's zone map and rate schedule for the specific plot. In growth areas, rates are often lower during construction phases to attract investment and are raised after infrastructure is complete — but check the current rate applies to your permit date.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is liable for komunalni doprinos on a new-build purchased off-plan?

The developer (investor) is liable. The contribution must be paid before the building permit is issued. Buyers should verify payment as part of due diligence.

Is komunalni doprinos refundable if construction does not proceed?

Municipalities may offer a partial refund if the permit lapses and no work is done, but this depends on local rules. Professional advice is needed in specific cases.

Does komunalni doprinos apply to renovation of existing buildings?

Major extensions that increase the usable floor area may attract a contribution. Internal refurbishments that do not change the floor area generally do not.
