Suglasnost Suvlasnika

Suglasnost suvlasnika is the consent of co-owners required under Croatian law before a jointly owned property can be sold, mortgaged, or significantly altered, protecting the rights of every party with a share in the asset.

Croatian property law treats co-ownership (suvlasništvo) as a relationship of mutual trust and limitation. No single co-owner may dispose of or encumber the entire property without the consent of all others. Suglasnost suvlasnika — literally 'co-owner consent' — is the written agreement that enables major decisions about the jointly held asset.

For the sale of the entire co-owned property, unanimous consent is required unless a court compels a sale in lieu of partition. For decisions about ordinary management of common property (e.g. minor repairs), a majority by ownership share suffices. For extraordinary management (structural works, leasing) a two-thirds majority is required.

Suglasnost must be documented in writing and, for land registry or notarial purposes, co-owners' signatures must typically be notarially certified. The consent should clearly identify the property, the nature of the transaction being authorised, and the terms agreed.

A buyer purchasing from one co-owner must ensure that other co-owners have first waived their right of first refusal (pravo prvokupa) and have provided any required suglasnost. Completing a transaction without these consents can expose the buyer to legal challenge.

If co-owners cannot agree, any of them may apply to the Municipal Court for a court order substituting for consent, or for a judgment ordering partition of the property — either physically or by sale with distribution of proceeds. Court-ordered sales are common in divorce and inheritance disputes.

⚠ Common MistakeBuyers sometimes accept a vendor's assurance that other co-owners have agreed verbally. Verbal consent is legally ineffective. Always demand notarially certified written consent from every co-owner before paying any deposit.

Frequently Asked Questions

What if one co-owner is deceased and their share has not been inherited?

The share passes to heirs through probate (ostavinski postupak). Until the estate is settled, the share cannot be sold. The solicitor must ensure the estate is administered and heirs identified before proceeding.

Can I buy only one co-owner's share?

Yes, subject to the other co-owners waiving their right of first refusal within 30 days of the formal offer notice.

Is suglasnost required for a mortgage on a co-owner's individual share?

Generally yes — a mortgage over jointly owned property requires all co-owners' consent. A mortgage only over one owner's undivided share requires their consent alone, but is less attractive to lenders.
