OIB (Osobni Identifikacijski Broj)

Croatia's 11-digit personal identification number — mandatory for all citizens and residents.

Overview

The OIB (Osobni Identifikacijski Broj — Personal Identification Number) is Croatia's mandatory 11-digit national identification number assigned to every citizen and resident. It serves as the primary identifier for all legal, tax, administrative, and financial transactions. The OIB is similar to personal identification numbers in other EU countries and is required for employment, tax filing, healthcare access, banking, property ownership, marriage registration, and government services.

Every Croatian citizen receives an OIB upon birth registration with the local civil registry (Matica Rođenih). Residents and foreign nationals working or doing business in Croatia are also assigned OIB numbers. The OIB is displayed on the Croatian national identity card (Osobna Iskaznica) and integrated into all major government databases and the property registration system.

The OIB structure includes a 10-digit base number plus a final check digit calculated using a weighted algorithm. The check digit validates the OIB's authenticity and most government and financial systems verify this automatically before accepting the number. Individuals cannot change or replace their OIB except for documented registration errors.

For property transactions in Croatia, both Croatian citizens and foreign buyers must register with their OIB or tax identification number (JMBG for some older citizens, or VAT identification number for businesses). The OIB is essential for mortgage applications, property registration with the Land Registry (Zemljišna Knjiga), and payment of property transfer taxes.

Croatia's transition to IBAN and EU integration has strengthened OIB requirements in financial systems. Online government services (e-Government Portal) increasingly use OIB for authentication and transaction authorization. Privacy is regulated under Croatian data protection law and GDPR, which restrict unauthorized use of OIB information.

Key Facts

  • 11-digit national identification number mandatory for citizens and residents
  • Required for all legal, tax, administrative, and business transactions
  • Issued by local civil registry (Matica Rođenih)
  • Displayed on national identity card (Osobna Iskaznica)
  • Integrated with tax authority, healthcare, and property systems

Common Mistake

Confusing OIB with JMBG (the older identification number still used by some citizens) — OIB is the current standard identifier.

Pro Tip

Verify OIB validity using the official check digit algorithm before submitting to government or financial institutions to avoid processing delays.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is OIB required for foreigners to buy property in Croatia?

Foreigners must have a tax identification number (usually VAT/UID number for non-residents, or OIB if obtaining residence status) for property transactions. The Land Registry requires this for registration of ownership.

Is JMBG the same as OIB?

No. JMBG (Jedinstveni Matični Broj Građana) was the previous Yugoslav identification number still held by some older citizens. OIB is the current Croatian standard. New citizens are issued OIB only.

What if my OIB is lost or stolen?

Report the loss to your local civil registry. You cannot change your OIB, but you can request a replacement identity card. The OIB itself remains valid and unchanged.
