Porez na Nekretnine

Porez na nekretnine is Croatia's annual property tax introduced in 2024, levied on all residential and commercial real estate owners based on the property's usable floor area and location, replacing the previous communal fee system.

Croatia introduced a modern annual property tax (porez na nekretnine) effective 1 January 2024, under the Zakon o porezu na nekretnine. The tax replaced the former komunalna naknada for residential and commercial properties and expanded the tax base to include all real estate, including holiday homes and investment properties that were previously lightly taxed.

The tax is calculated on the property's usable floor area (m²) multiplied by a value per square metre set by the municipality, adjusted by the property's location within a zone (zona nekretnina) and its classification (residential, commercial, agricultural). Municipalities set rates within bounds defined by national law, creating geographic variation in the annual liability.

Primary residences (primary home classification) benefit from a mandatory 50% reduction on the calculated tax liability. Additional reductions are available for pensioners with low incomes, properties under renovation, and agricultural land classifications. The Croatian Tax Administration (Porezna uprava) collects the tax annually.

All property owners were required to register their properties with the Tax Administration and submit data on usable floor area and use by the deadline set by the Ministry of Finance in 2024. Properties registered in the land registry are automatically notified, but unregistered or informally occupied properties required manual declaration.

Non-payment of the annual property tax creates a debt enforceable by the Tax Administration, including against the property itself via a tax lien. Buyers must request a tax compliance certificate from the vendor to ensure no outstanding property tax liabilities exist before completion.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does porez na nekretnine apply to vacant land?

Agricultural land classified as such is taxed at a lower rate or exempt. Undeveloped urban plots are generally taxable. Confirm the specific classification with the Tax Administration.

Are holiday homes taxed differently?

Yes. Properties not declared as primary residence are taxed at the full rate without the 50% primary-home reduction, making annual liabilities higher for second homes.

How often must the tax be paid?

Annually. The Tax Administration issues an assessment notice; payment is typically due within 15 days of the notice, though advance instalments may be arranged.
